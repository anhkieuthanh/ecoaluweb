# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary audience: B2B procurement managers and buyers at domestic and international manufacturers — automotive, construction, aerospace, electronics — evaluating a bulk aluminum supplier. They arrive with specific questions: Can this factory deliver at the volume I need? What certifications do they hold? Can I trust them as a long-term partner?

Secondary audiences: investors and strategic partners evaluating EcoAlu's scale and ESG track record; press and government bodies assessing the factory's industrial and environmental impact.

The surface (ecoalu.net) speaks to external stakeholders, not internal users. Every screen should pass the "first impression" test of an international procurement manager who has never heard of EcoAlu.

## Product Purpose

EcoAlu is a high-tech aluminum factory in Thanh Hoa, Vietnam, producing Ingot (30,000 tons/year), Billet (70,000 tons/year), and Profile products using Japanese manufacturing technology. The website's purpose is to convert qualified B2B visitors — who arrive skeptical — into procurement inquiries by establishing industrial credibility, communicating certified quality, and differentiating EcoAlu as Southeast Asia's leading circular-economy aluminum producer.

Success looks like: a procurement manager submits a contact form, downloads a product spec sheet, or picks up the phone after seeing the site.

## Positioning

Vietnam's first high-tech, circular-economy aluminum factory delivering international-grade product at industrial scale.

## Conversion & proof

- **Primary CTA**: Contact for a quote (Liên hệ / Contact form)
- **Secondary CTA**: View product specifications / download brochure (for visitors not yet ready to commit)
- **The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds**: "100,000-ton-per-year capacity. Japanese technology. ISO certified. Vietnam's answer to international aluminum demand."
- **Belief ladder** — what a visitor must believe before submitting a contact form:
  1. EcoAlu is a real, operational, large-scale factory (not a trading company or early-stage project)
  2. Their product quality is certified to international standards (ISO 9001, ISO 14001, JIS H4100, RoHS)
  3. They have the technical capability to fulfill complex, spec-sensitive orders (Japanese technology, continuous-casting line, multiple alloy grades)
  4. They are the responsible, future-proof choice in the region (circular economy, ESG commitments, carbon credentials)
  5. → It is worth initiating contact
- **Proof on hand**: partner and client logos (public/images/partners/), ISO and JIS certifications, press coverage and awards, operational statistics (capacity, headcount, years in operation) in Stats section

## Brand Personality

Precise, Innovative, Sustainable. The brand speaks with the confidence of an industrial authority — not a commodity supplier, not a startup pitching its vision. Tone is direct and technical when presenting specs, warmer and aspirational when presenting ESG and Vietnam's industrial future. Never promotional-sounding; never vague.

Reference: Stavian Metal's aesthetic direction — modern, luxurious, minimal corporate industrial — applied to EcoAlu's distinct green + deep forest identity.

## Anti-references

**Generic Vietnamese corporate**: cluttered layouts, banner-heavy hero, low-contrast text on gradient backgrounds, excessive decorative elements. EcoAlu should feel like a step-change above the Vietnamese industrial web norm.

**Greenwashing SaaS**: leaf icons, stock photos of forests and hands holding plants, surface-level ESG language with no data behind it. EcoAlu's green credentials are real and specific — show them through certifications, metrics, and process, not iconography.

## Design Principles

**Credibility through evidence, not assertion.** Show real numbers (30,000 / 70,000 tons/year), real factory photography, real certifications, real partner logos. Every screen answers a procurement question. Claims without backing are invisible to B2B buyers.

**Industrial precision as aesthetic.** The design system should feel as precise as the manufacturing process — tight grids, exact spacing, strong typographic hierarchy. Ornament earns its place or gets cut.

**Green earned, not performed.** ESG presence through specific data and named certifications only. Color and imagery signal the identity; the copy and proof close the case. Never a leaf where a metric belongs.

**International legibility.** The site must read clearly to a Japanese, European, or Southeast Asian procurement manager on first visit — clear navigation, fast orientation, no cultural in-jokes or local-only references that confuse a foreign buyer.

**Scale signals authority.** Every section's layout and visual weight should reinforce that this is a hundred-thousand-ton operation, not a workshop. Generous spacing, full-bleed factory imagery, and confident typography over dense text.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Trilingual support (VI / EN / CN) is critical — the site must be fully functional and readable in all three languages including CJK rendering. Reduced motion alternative required for all Framer Motion animations. Sufficient contrast on all body text against backgrounds, including the dark (#041221) sections where light text is used.
